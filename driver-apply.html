Terms & Conditions

Effective Date: May 1, 2026 | Jurisdiction: Ontario, Canada

1. Independent Contractor Relationship

Participation in the Roadside Intelligence Network ("RIN") driver network does not create an employment relationship. Drivers are independent contractors. RIN does not control your hours, routes, methods of work, or equipment. You are responsible for your own taxes, WSIB coverage (if applicable), and business licensing.

2. Platform Fee and Payout

RIN charges a platform fee of 10% on each completed job. Drivers receive 90% of the customer-confirmed job total. The payout amount is confirmed to the driver before job acceptance. No additional fees, deductions, or chargebacks are applied after the driver accepts a job, except in cases of driver-initiated cancellation on scene.

3. Payment Terms

Payments are processed within 24 hours of job completion and customer confirmation. Payment is made to the account or method on file in your driver profile. RIN is not liable for delays caused by incorrect banking information provided by the driver.

4. Job Acceptance and Refusal

You are under no obligation to accept any dispatched job. Accepting a job creates a binding commitment to complete the service unless a valid reason prevents completion. If you cannot complete an accepted job, you must notify RIN dispatch immediately. Repeated no-shows or late cancellations may result in suspension from the network.

5. Driver-Cancelled Jobs

If you cancel after arriving on scene, no compensation is paid. The customer will be notified and reassigned. RIN reserves the right to investigate repeated cancellations and suspend drivers who misuse this provision.

6. Price Amendments

If additional work is required on scene, you may request a price amendment through the RIN dispatch system. The amended price is subject to customer approval before work continues. You must wait for customer approval before proceeding with any work that differs from the original quoted scope.

7. Equipment and Licensing

You represent and warrant that you hold all required licences, certifications, and insurance to operate your vehicle and perform towing services in Ontario, including any applicable MTO/TSSEA requirements. RIN may request proof of certification at any time.

8. TSSEA Compliance

All jobs dispatched through RIN are conducted in accordance with Ontario's Towing and Storage Safety and Enforcement Act (TSSEA). Drivers are responsible for their own compliance with TSSEA operational requirements.

9. WhatsApp and Electronic Communications

By participating in the network, you agree to receive job dispatch notifications via SMS. WhatsApp communications are subject to separate, explicit consent as collected in your application. You may withdraw consent to any communication channel by contacting RIN at 647-473-1338.

10. Network Suspension and Termination

RIN reserves the right to suspend or remove any driver from the network for cause, including but not limited to: repeated cancellations, customer complaints, licence violations, TSSEA non-compliance, or misrepresentation of qualifications. Notice will be provided where reasonably possible.

11. Limitation of Liability

RIN is a dispatch platform and is not liable for accidents, property damage, personal injury, or losses arising from a driver's performance of towing services. Each driver maintains their own commercial auto insurance and general liability coverage.

12. Governing Law

These Terms are governed by the laws of the Province of Ontario and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein. Any disputes shall be resolved in the courts of Ontario.

13. Amendments

RIN may update these Terms from time to time. Continued participation in the network following notification of changes constitutes acceptance of the updated Terms.

Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 1, 2026 | Compliant with PIPEDA and CASL | Ontario, Canada

1. Who We Are

Roadside Intelligence Network ("RIN") operates a technology-enabled tow dispatch platform in Ontario, Canada. This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through the RIN driver application process, driver profile management, and dispatch operations.

2. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Identity information: Full name, phone number, email address
  • Professional information: Driver type, truck types, service area, years of experience, MTO certification status
  • Operational data: Job acceptance/decline history, payout records, GPS data during active jobs (with your knowledge)
  • Communication consent records: CASL consent timestamps, WhatsApp group consent records (if provided)
  • Technical data: Device type, browser, page referrer (standard web analytics)

3. How We Use Your Information

Your information is used for the following purposes:

  • Processing your driver application and onboarding
  • Dispatching job opportunities to your phone
  • Processing payments and maintaining payment records
  • Communicating network updates, rate changes, and operational notices
  • Complying with MTO/TSSEA regulatory requirements
  • Improving dispatch accuracy and platform performance

4. WhatsApp Group Communications

If you consented during application to allow RIN to share information with a WhatsApp group you are a member of, the following applies:

  • Your consent is specific, time-stamped, and recorded in our system
  • A maximum of one introductory message will be sent to the group
  • No automated or repeated messages will be sent without renewed explicit consent
  • You may revoke this consent at any time by contacting us at 647-473-1338
  • RIN complies fully with Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation (CASL) for all electronic communications

5. SMS and Email Communications (CASL)

If you provided express consent to commercial electronic messages, RIN may send SMS or email communications about job opportunities, platform updates, and network changes. Each message includes an unsubscribe mechanism. To withdraw consent, reply STOP to any SMS or contact us at 647-473-1338.

6. Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We share your information only with:

  • Technology service providers necessary to operate our platform (Supabase, Twilio) under data processing agreements
  • Customers: only your first name and ETA are shared during an active dispatch — never your contact information
  • Regulatory bodies when required by law (MTO, TSSEA enforcement)

7. Data Retention

Driver application records are retained for a minimum of 7 years for regulatory compliance purposes. Active driver profiles are retained for the duration of the driver's participation in the network plus 2 years. You may request deletion of non-regulatory data at any time.

8. Your Rights Under PIPEDA

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Correct inaccurate information
  • Withdraw consent to non-essential uses
  • Request deletion of personal data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Lodge a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada

9. Security

Your personal information is stored in encrypted databases hosted in Canada. Access is restricted to authorized RIN personnel only. We apply industry-standard security practices including role-based access control and audit logging.

10. Contact

For any privacy inquiries, access requests, or consent withdrawals, contact RIN at 647-473-1338. We respond to all privacy requests within 30 days.
