Common Questions
Before the call.
What is the per-incident rate?›
The rate is confirmed on the 30-minute call based on your fleet profile and vehicle types. It's a flat rate per dispatch — no variable add-ons. The rate is locked in the pilot agreement before you go live.
Does my team need to download anything or change how they work?›
No. Your driver or dispatcher texts a single number. That's it. No app to install, no account to create, no software training. The change for your team is one new contact saved in their phone.
What happens if RIN can't find a tow fast enough?›
If we can't confirm a tower within the SLA window, you're notified immediately with the status and we escalate. During the pilot, you also have a direct escalation line. We don't go silent on an open job.
How long does the pilot run?›
The pilot runs for 60 days. At the end, you decide whether to continue on a standard agreement or part ways — no obligation either way. There's no auto-renewal and no cancellation fee.
What if we only have 2–3 incidents during the pilot?›
That's fine. You pay for what happens. Low-volume months are low-cost months. The pilot is designed to demonstrate the workflow even on a small number of jobs — most fleets see the value by the second or third incident.
