Common Questions
Before the call.
Does RIN dispatch into my MTO contract zone or just outside it?›
RIN handles both. When a job falls inside your MTO contract zone and your trucks are available, it goes to your fleet at your government rate. When your trucks are committed, RIN can dispatch a network driver at the commercial rate. You control which jobs get routed where — the platform doesn't override your contract obligations.
What happens to my government contract rates in the system?›
You enter your government contract rates in the portal under the Government Contract tab. Rates are stored per zone and per highway. The portal also pulls the current MTO rate schedule from Ontario Open Data monthly so you can compare your filed rates against the published reference — it's for your reference only; your filed rates control what appears on your invoices.
Can I decline jobs from the RIN network if I don't want them?›
Yes. If you're on Platform + Network, overflow jobs are only dispatched when your own drivers are unavailable. There's no obligation to accept every job the network surfaces. The 20% commission only applies to jobs you actually run through the RIN network — nothing is charged for jobs you turn down or that go elsewhere.
We already use a dispatch system. Does RIN replace it?›
That depends on what your current system does. Most of the MTO operators we've spoken with are running dispatch from a phone and a spreadsheet — RIN replaces that. If you have a more formal TMS, we'd want to understand what you actually need before the call so we're not overselling replacement of something that works.
How does billing work at the end of the month?›
Your billing dashboard shows every transaction for the month: which jobs ran with your own drivers (no commission), which ran through the RIN network (20% commission shown), gross per job, and your net payout. One invoice at month end. Everything is itemized so your bookkeeper can verify each line without calling us.
What's the monthly platform fee?›
The platform fee is confirmed on the 30-minute call based on your fleet size, zone coverage, and which plan you choose. We don't publish a rate card because the number of trucks and zones varies enough that a flat posted price would be wrong for most operators. You leave the call with the number in writing before you agree to anything.
