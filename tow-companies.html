FAQ
Questions we hear a lot.
What does RIN take per job? +
RIN keeps 20% of the job value as a platform fee. You receive 80%, paid within 7 days. On a $260 standard tow, you get $208. No other deductions, no monthly fee, no hidden costs.
Do I need to change how I dispatch my drivers? +
No. RIN sends the job offer to your dispatch phone via SMS. You decide who does the job internally — that's your business. We only need the SMS replies: YES to accept, ARRIVED on site, DONE when complete.
What if I can't take a job when it comes in? +
Reply NO or simply don't reply within 2 minutes — RIN will move to the next operator in your zone. No penalty for declining. We understand your trucks are sometimes tied up.
What types of fleet vehicles will I be towing? +
Our pilot fleet clients operate transport trucks, cube vans, flatbeds, and tankers across Ontario. Most jobs are standard semi breakdowns — blown tires, engine failures, accidents. Your truck type eligibility determines which job types you receive.
How many jobs will I realistically get per month? +
During the Q2 2026 pilot, volume starts small — expect 3–8 jobs/month per zone as fleet clients onboard. Volume grows as we add fleet clients. We'd rather under-promise and over-deliver than flood you with jobs you can't handle.
What if the fleet customer disputes the job or the charge? +
RIN locks the rate before you move. The fleet client authorizes payment before dispatch confirms. By the time you're rolling, payment is guaranteed. You're not carrying any credit risk. If a dispute occurs on the fleet side, that's between RIN and the fleet — your payout is protected.
